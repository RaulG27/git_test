New York-Style Pizza

Description

New York-style pizza is known for its thin, foldable crust, large slices, and simple yet flavorful toppings like tomato sauce and whole milk mozzarella cheese. These pizzas are often cooked in very hot ovens, which results in a slightly charred and crispy bottom, while the slices are served in a classic "fold and eat" style. New York-style pizza is a beloved, iconic dish available both by the slice and as whole pies, emphasizing the quality of ingredients and a balance of flavors, with variations like white pizza and Sicilian pizza also in the mix.

Ingredients

  • Pizza Dough: Thin and hand-tossed for a crispy yet foldable crust
  • Tomato Sauce: Simple and flavorful tomato sauce to spred on the dough
  • Whole Milk Mozzarella Cheese: preferably shredded
  • Olive Oil: perfect for the crispy finish
  • Flour: you'll need flour for dusting the pizza peel or pan to prevent sticking

Steps

  1. Prepare Dough: Roll out pizza dough into a thin and round shape
  2. Spread Sauce: Evenly spread tomato sauce over the dough, leaving a small border
  3. Add Cheese: Cover the sauce with whole milk mozzarella cheese
  4. Bake: Pre-heat at 490Â°F Then place the pizza in the oven on a pan and bake until the crush is golden and the cheese is visibly bubbly
  5. Serve: Remove the pizza from the pan, add olive oil and cut the slices as you desire and enjoy your New York-Style Pizza!
